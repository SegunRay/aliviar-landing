Cover, explained simply

Five ways to protect what your income and home depend on.

Every situation is different — we'll help you work out which of these actually matter for you, and skip the ones that don't.

Life insurance

A lump sum paid to your family if you die during the policy term — so a mortgage, bills or your children's future aren't left to chance.

Income protection

Replaces part of your salary if illness or injury stops you working — paid monthly for as long as you need it, until you're back on your feet.

Serious illness protection

Pays out a tax‑free lump sum on diagnosis of a condition like cancer, a heart attack or stroke — money to focus on recovery, not finances.

Mortgage protection

Cover built around your mortgage balance and term, so if the worst happens, your home doesn't become a financial burden for those left behind.

Buildings & contents

Protects the structure of your home and everything in it — from storm damage to theft — so an accident doesn't become a financial crisis.

Not sure what you need?

Most people only need two or three of these. Tell us a bit about your situation and we'll tell you honestly what's worth having.
